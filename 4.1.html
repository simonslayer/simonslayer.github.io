Chapter 4 - Networks and the effects of using them

Sub-chapter 4.1 - Networks

A network is when a computer is connected to a multitude of other computers. An example of this could include work computers from the office sharing a network with a resource such as a printer or software. The basic use of a network is to allow computers to communicate with others.

There are many devices that take use of networks, wether it would be to create them or simply use them. To name a few;

    Modems
    Hubs
    Bridges
    Routers


Every device on the internet is given an IP address (Internet Protocal address). This address is a 32-bit number written in the form;

    109.108.158.1

Wifi and Bluetooth creates a form of wireless communication between different devices. Wifi recieves its information via a broadband connection while bluetooth recieves radio waves with a sound of 79 frequencies centered around a 2.45GHz frequency.
